THE DANCES

Competitive Ballroom and Latin Dance

The Dancesport Club at UCLA is the home of dancesport at UCLA. Dancesport is competitive Ballroom and Latin dancing. We are a registered Student Organization with SOLE at University of California, Los Angeles. As a team we represent UCLA at regional competitions from San Diego to San Francisco and Arizona to the ocean. No experience or partner is required to join us! We are open to everyone. Dancesport is broken down into two main styles: International Ballroom and International Latin dance.

International Ballroom

Originating in European countries of England and Austria, as well as the underground of Argentina, this style is the classic Ballroom you may have seen in such movies as Anastasia or Harry Potter. Smoother and classier than Latin, Ballroom consists of Waltz, Tango, Viennese Waltz, Slow Foxtrot and Quickstep.

In your first quarter with DCU, Beginning Standard (Fridays at 7:30) introduces members to Waltz and Quickstep, the easiest dancest to compete as a beginner, both taught from the very very beginning.

Intermediate Ballroom (Saturdays at 2:00) is intended for dancers with at least 6 months of Dancesport experience and will focus on expanding the Bronze syllabus in all 5 dances with a special emphasis on techniques and an intermediate level of competition readiness.

Standard Drills (Saturdays at 1:00) provides special training in the techniques and body movements that make Ballroom so precise. Designed for those who have completed at least 3 months of Beginning Ballroom.

International Latin

Originating in the Latin American countries of Brazil and Argentina, this style has been formulated and codified into the Latin dance style competed today, which you may recognize from Dirty Dancing or Dancing with the Stars. Recognizable for its sensual hip action and upbeat music, this style is divided into 5 dances: Cha Cha, Samba, Rumba, Paso Doble and Jive.

In your first quarter at DCU, Beginning Latin (Fridays at 6:30) will cover Cha Cha and Rumba, the easiest dances to try as a complete beginner and will teach them from the very basics.

Intermediate Latin (Fridays at 5:30) is designed for dancers with at least 6 months of Dancesport experience and will focus on expanding the Bronze syllabus for all 5 dances to allow dancers a more expansive repertoire of movement for competition as well as focusing on techniques and strategies that will be vital for victory at competition.

Latin Partnering (Saturdays at 3:00) allows all levels of dance partnership to get more specific training in the intricacies of dancesport partnering for Latin. This is the ONLY course for which a dance partner is required for all class sessions.
